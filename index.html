My Story

From CSE Graduate to
Enterprise Integration Engineer

A journey through BPM, EAI, and data — building systems that connect, automate, and inform.

I graduated B.Tech in Computer Science with a CGPA of 9.01 from Lakireddy Bali Reddy College of Engineering, where I built a foundation in data structures, algorithms, and software engineering. My real-world awakening came through enterprise platforms — specifically Pega, where I earned both my CSA and CSSA certifications and learned that software is more than code; it is process.

Selected by TCS through Pega, I was placed in the EAI (Enterprise Application Integration) domain under the CBG Cluster. My ILP training at TCS ESU, Hyderabad (Adibatla) centers on TIBCO BusinessWorks — designing integration flows, configuring JMS and HTTP connections, building REST/SOAP services, and understanding how large-scale systems communicate.

"Enterprises don't just need code — they need integration. The ability to make disparate systems speak to each other reliably, at scale, in real time."

Alongside my integration journey, I apply Power BI and Python to turn raw data into business narratives. From building end-to-end ETL pipelines and DAX-driven dashboards to training ML models for healthcare detection, I believe that data without visualization is noise.

My target: grow into a Senior Integration Architect who can design API-first, event-driven enterprise systems — and use data analytics to measure and improve them.

Design Philosophy

How I Design
Enterprise Systems

My approach to building integration-heavy, data-driven enterprise solutions — drawing from EAI, BPM, and API-first principles.

🔌

API-First Design

I define contracts before implementation. Every integration begins with an API spec — OpenAPI-driven, version-aware, and consumer-centric.

⚡

Event-Driven Architecture

Leveraging asynchronous messaging (JMS, message brokers) to decouple producers and consumers — enabling resilience and real-time responsiveness.

📐

Scalable Integration Patterns

Applying enterprise integration patterns — content-based routing, splitter/aggregator, message enrichment — to design middleware flows at scale.

📊

Data Pipeline Thinking

From raw ingestion through transformation to presentation — I design data flows that are idempotent, observable, and aligned to business KPIs.
